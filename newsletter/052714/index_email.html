Ganglion Cysts:
Tumors of the Hand
Dr. Joe Morgan

Ganglion cysts are the most common tumors of the hand. They are benign tumors. They can arise from a joint or from certain tendons. They occur when fluid leaks out of either the joint or the covering of the tendon. The fluid becomes encapsulated and forms a nodularity that usually can be palpated. Cysts usually occur spontaneously but they have been known to occur after repetitive stress type activities involving the hand. Cysts in the palm of the hand that are associated with tendons can be palpated easily. If the cyst in the wrist is large enough it can be palpated. Occasionally there will be small cysts that we call occult cysts. To help diagnose occult cysts MRI or ultrasound can be used. Cysts can either be painful or painless. There is also a cyst that can occur in the finger at the joint very close to the fingernail. Those cysts are called mucocysts. Mucocysts are created by degenerative arthritis in the joint. The definitive treatment for all cysts is surgical excision.
