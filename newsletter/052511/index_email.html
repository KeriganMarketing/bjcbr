Meet William Sherman, Jr., M.D.

Dr. Sherman is a native of Louisiana and received his Bachelor's and Medical Degree from Louisiana State University. He completed his residency in orthopedic surgery at LSU in New Orleans and was the recipient of numerous awards including the Resident of the Year award as a Chief Resident.

He was fellowship trained in Adult Reconstruction at Harvard focusing on hip and knee replacements, including primary procedures and complex revision cases.

His interests include advanced hip reconstruction, hip resurfacing, partial knee replacements, primary total hip & knee replacements, revision knee arthroplasty, and knee realignment surgery involving arthritis.

He performs all aspects of hip reconstruction utilizing multiple approaches including the anterior hip approach. This diverse expertise allows for personal customization of each surgery depending on individual patient needs.

He also practices general orthopedics including hand surgery, sports medicine, and orthopedic trauma.

In Season:
Myths about 'Warming Up' Summer Muscles

For healthy living you still need to exercise during summer. But is it really necessary to "warm-up" when the temperature outside is already so hot? The truth is that the air temperature outdoors is not a determining factor of whether or not your muscles and joints are ready for a summer workout. Instead, your body needs proper stretching and warm-up exercises in order to prepare for strenuous physical activity, no matter what the temperature is outside.

According to the American Academy of Orthopedic Surgeons, "Warming up increases your heart and blood flow rates and loosens up other muscles, tendons, ligaments, and joints." Without stretching and warm-up exercises, your body can be put at risk for joint or muscle-related injury. The AAOS says that, "Warming up gives your body time to adjust to the demands of exercise. This can improve your performance and help you get the results you want."

A proper warm-up increases your cardiorespiratory system's ability to do work. It also makes you body more efficient in the amount of oxygen that it is able to absorb and utilize. So, even during the hot summer months, your body still needs to warm up. As you exercise, your body temperature increases, and likewise, blood flow improves. When your muscles receive an increase of blood flow, they become more elastic and ready for physical activity
